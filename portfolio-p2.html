PHOTOSHOOT

16. Jan. 2020

This product photoshoot was really heavily dependent on Photoshop. I wanted to have my object on a dark background so, I grabbed a dark towel for this. I had two lamps as lighting from both sides, which will help with lighting up the liquid and not make it blend with the dark background. I then took the splashes and contrasted the background so I can then overlay it without any detail (didn't want the towel to appear in the splashes). I then used a red layer to apply it over the splashes and make it red through layer effects. I then cut out the product and warped the splash images around the bottle. I then added some red reflections to the bottle, added more colour to the splashes, and finally added a dust layer along with a flare and used layer effects to add some spice to the picture.

5. May 2018

These are a few pictures I took at Durdle doors. These are all panoramas. I didn't have a wide-angle lens, and I knew they are the best for landscape photography. During this, the main thing I would change is planning. Usually, when people go to known landscapes, they mindlessly take pictures of the place. I think the best way to improve is by placing a subject in the scene or thinking of a better composition instead of including everything in the shot. One of the main reasons photographers don't like wide-angle lenses is because there's not as much creativity and thought when it comes to composition. Everything gets captured in the photo.

PHOTOSHOOT

1. Oct. 2017

When I first got my DSLR, I wasn't thinking about how well it performed and wasn't too critical about the image quality. I watched a lot of videos on how to change settings, so I used manual settings from the start along with autofocus. Before getting my camera, I have watched a few videos on editing with Lightroom. I decided to get Lightroom and combine both tools to create better images. I used the radial tool a lot and changed the temperature to bring separation and contrast to the pictures, which I liked back then. MG_2386 is one of my best images back then. I used the rule of thirds as a technique to improve my photos. The image 'MG_3185' was taken in November. I started using other features from Lightroom and really started to learn the software as a whole. I was excited about this result, and even now, I think this is a pretty well shot and edited photo.
